OUR FOUNDER

Who is Rhanderson
Cardoso, MD, FACC?

A cardiologist at Brigham and Women’s Hospital, Harvard Medical School, with cardiology training and a Master’s degree in Cardiovascular Epidemiology from Johns Hopkins.

The Journey of Rhanderson Cardoso

From Brazil to Harvard.

Tocantins, Brazil

The Origin.

Dr. Rhanderson Cardoso was born in Tocantins, Brazil, where his values of faith, resilience, and service to others were first shaped. These early years laid the foundation for a life dedicated to medicine, education, and global impact.

Miami, United States

Chief Resident

University of Miami

He moved to the United States to complete his medical residency at the University of Miami, where he also served as chief resident. Dr. Cardoso distinguished himself as both a physician and educator — earning a prestigious award established in his own name.

Boston, United States

Harvard Medical School

Harvard Medical School

Currently based in Boston at Brigham and Women's Hospital, Harvard Medical School, Dr. Cardoso continues to combine clinical excellence with academic leadership. With over 100 publications and more than 2,650 citations, his work has influenced international cardiovascular guidelines and inspired physicians worldwide.
